## Objectives
The ISO 27005 defines the following objectives
- Identify risks (Important, obvious, and Hidden risks)
- Ensure activities and processes are designed to manage risks
- Respond to risks and reduce risk exposure
- Establish a risk action plan

## 9. Negative risk treatment
- The origin of these risks are the weaknesses in **SWOT** analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
- Depending on risk appetite, define how the risk will be onboarded

| Strategy     | Description                                                                                                                      |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Reduce**   | Apply certain actions to reduce the level of risk                                                                                |
| **Accept**   | Decide to deal with the risk (Take No action) depending on the organization expectation. Usually risks that can't be controlled. |
| **Avoid**    | The activity or action that leads to this risk is avoided.                                                                       |
| **Transfer** | Offload the risk to a third party (e.g. service providers)                                                                       |
| **Share**    | Offload part of the risk to a third party (e.g. service providers)                                                               |

## 10. Positive risk treatment

| Strategy    | Description                                                      |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Exploit** | Reduce uncertainty, search for ways to increase success          |
| **Share**   | Start involving third parties increase the likelihood of success |
| **Improve** | Work the conditions that originate this risk                     |
| **Accept**  | Take no action until the opportunity is landed                   |

# Evaluating the Risk Management Strategy

- Risk management consist of three phases
- 1. Identifying risks
	- Calculating the Risk Appetite (Initial risk)
- 2. Responding to risks
	- Recalculating the Risk Appetite (Residual risk)
- 3. Evaluating from Initial risk and determining residual risk
