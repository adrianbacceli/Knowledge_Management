# Module 2 - Define and Identify Risks and Threats

## Deep Dive into ISO 27005

This document is intended to help define, identify, assess, and treat risks in a structured and repeatable way.

---

## 1. Objectives

ISO 27005 supports the following objectives:

- Identify risks, including obvious, important, and hidden risks.
- Ensure activities and processes are designed to manage risks.
- Respond to risks and reduce risk exposure.
- Establish a risk action plan.

## 11. Negative Risk Treatment

Negative risks typically originate from weaknesses identified during **SWOT analysis**:

- **Strengths**
- **Weaknesses**
- **Opportunities**
- **Threats**

Depending on the organization's risk appetite, define how each risk will be treated.

| Strategy | Description |
| -------- | ----------- |
| **Reduce** | Apply actions to reduce the level of risk. |
| **Accept** | Take no action and consciously live with the risk, usually when it cannot be controlled or the cost of treatment is too high. |
| **Avoid** | Avoid the activity or action that leads to the risk. |
| **Transfer** | Offload the risk to a third party (for example, a service provider or insurer). |
| **Share** | Offload part of the risk to a third party. |

# Part D - Evaluating the Risk Management Strategy

Risk management consists of three main phases:

## 15. Phase 1 - Identify Risks

- Identify the relevant risks.
- Calculate the **initial risk**.
- Compare the risk against the defined appetite.

## 16. Phase 2 - Respond to Risks

- Define and apply treatment actions.
- Recalculate the risk after treatment.
- Determine the **residual risk**.

## 17. Phase 3 - Evaluate Results

- Compare **initial risk** vs **residual risk**.
- Confirm whether the treatment reduced risk sufficiently.
- Identify remaining gaps, open actions, or follow-up items.
