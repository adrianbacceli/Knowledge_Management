## 4.3 Determine the Scope

1. Use 4.1 for context and 4.2 for requirements
2. Determine the scope with the following criteria ![[Pasted image 20260408193024.png]]
	1. Grab list of services and products 
	2. Define list of process/services 
	3. Define the location
	4. Define the networks and Infrastructure (Network Diagram) that includes the information roadmap based on previous defined criteria (1., 2., and 3.)![[Pasted image 20260408193241.png]]
	5. Tools that support services and processes
		1. X tool is used for Z service (e.g. CRM for customer services)
	6. Providers (ISPs, HVAC, DC, AV, Firewalls, Licenses, Etc.)
	7. Exclusions (Out of Scope) + SoA

# 5 Leadership![[Pasted image 20260408195310.png]]

---
## 5.1 Leadership and Commitment
- Ensure that Information Security policies and objectives are established
- Ensure requirements and processes are defined (4.2 and 4.4)
- Ensure resources availability
- Communicate importance of adequate management
- Ensure results achievement
- Drive and support people through efficiency
- Promote continuous improvement
- Support other lores in management

---
## 5.2 Policy
- It's aligned to organization's purpose (Mission)
- Includes Information Security objectives
- Includes commitment to satisfy SoA and requirements
- Includes commitment to constant improvement


> [!NOTE] Policy requirements and considerations
> - It should be available as a document
> - It should be communicated internally
> - It should be available to Interested Parties

---
## 5.3 Roles, Responsibilities, Organizational Authorities

- Ensure adequate responsibilities and roles
- Ensure the ISMS is compliant to legal requirements
- Report in performance and results to Leadership
