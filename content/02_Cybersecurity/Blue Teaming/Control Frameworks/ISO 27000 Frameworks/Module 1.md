# Principles

---
## Data Core Pilars: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability

* The ISO 27001 is based under [[CIA Principles|CIA Triad]] that ensure data is always available, information was not altered unwantedly, and privacy is maintained.

---
## Authentication

* Authenticity is the assurance that a person, system, or piece of data is truly who or what it claims to be. It answers the question, _"Who are you?"_ and verifies your identity using credentials (passwords, tokens, or biometrics).

---
## Authorization

* This happens after authenticity is established. It determines, _"What are you allowed to do?"_ (e.g., read-only access vs. admin rights).

---
## Accountability (Non Repudiation)

- It tracks what a user did and when they did it. Without the detailed logs and time-stamping provided by Accounting, you wouldn't have the forensic evidence to prove an action occurred.

---
## Privacy

* It's the concept of establishing which data can be shared with third party. Establishing controls for data sharing and access to be provided.

> [!question] Did you know?
>  60% of companies that suffer a cybersecurity breach end up closing within 6 months.

* **Information Security Officer (ISO)**

	* Security Investigation

## ISO 27035:2022 

It helps build the Incident Management Plan. It should be incorporated gradually in the organization
- Plan and preparation
- Detection and report
- Evaluation and decision
- Response 
- Lessons learned

Defines Roles, processes, and incidents.
* Incident: Unwanted Events that jeopardize security
* Disaster: Critical Events that affect or risks business continuity
