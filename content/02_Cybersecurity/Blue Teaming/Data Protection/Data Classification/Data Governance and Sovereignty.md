## 🧭 Data Governance and Quality

> [!info]  
> **Data governance** ensures that data remains private, accurate, secure, available, and usable. It defines how data is managed throughout its lifecycle using roles, policies, and processes.

### 👥 Key Roles and Concepts

- **Data Stewards**: Experts who maintain data quality and integrity  
- **Governance Framework**: Policies for data access, usage, storage, and disposal, aligned with laws and standards  

### 📊 Dimensions of Data Quality

| Dimension     | Description                                  |
|---------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Accuracy      | Matches a trusted source                     |
| Completeness  | Sufficient for decisions                     |
| Consistency   | Aligned across systems                       |
| Timeliness    | Available when needed                        |
| Validity      | Meets formatting or domain expectations      |
| Uniqueness    | No unnecessary duplicates                    |

> [!tip]  
> Maintain data quality by defining policies, securing access, and auditing regularly.

### ✅ Benefits
- Supports compliance and data-driven decisions  
- Increases trust and reduces risk  
- Maintains high data integrity

## 🌍 Data Sovereignty and Governance in the Cloud

> [!info]  
> As cloud adoption grows, understanding **where** data resides is critical for legal and compliance reasons.

### 🌐 Key Concepts

| Term              | Meaning                                                                 |
|-------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Data Sovereignty**  | Data must comply with the laws of its storage location              |
| **Data Residency**    | Geographical location of stored data                                |
| **Data Localization** | Law requiring local data storage                                    |

> [!note]  
> Sovereignty laws vary by country—cloud providers must support region-specific controls.

### 🛠️ Governance Actions
- Conduct data audits  
- Choose providers with regional support  
- Map sovereignty requirements and align policies  

### 🎯 Benefits
- Reduces regulatory risk  
- Ensures lawful data storage and handling  
- Supports operational resilience and transparency

## 🔍 Data Discovery

> [!info]  
> **Data discovery** involves identifying and analyzing data across the organization to uncover insights, assess risk, and support governance.

### 📌 Key Concepts

| Concept            | Description                                                   |
|--------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| Data Discovery     | Locating and analyzing data across systems                    |
| Data Governance    | Ensuring lawful, secure data management                       |
| Data Sovereignty   | Legal compliance with storage regulations                     |

> [!warning]  
> **Sensitive Data Discovery**:  
> - Focus on PII, financial records, PHI, IP  
> - Tools: DLP engines (scan, mask, classify)  
> - Ensure encryption, access control, and retention compliance  

### 💡 Example Use Cases

- Financial institutions mask sensitive data in email  
- Healthcare providers protect PHI during cloud migration  
- Multinationals enforce sovereignty by region using audits and encryption  

### ✅ Benefits
- Enhances visibility and transparency  
- Validates secure data storage in the cloud  
- Reduces misclassification and governance gaps

## 🗂️ Data Retention

> [!info]  
> **Data retention** refers to storing data for a defined period to meet legal, regulatory, or business needs.

### 📌 Core Concepts

| Concept               | Description                                                |
|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Data Retention         | Storing data for a specific duration                      |
| Retention Period       | Defined storage timeline                                  |
| Data Retention Policy  | Rules governing storage, access, and disposal             |

> [!check]  
> Steps to create a data retention policy:
> - Research legal and industry requirements  
> - Consult legal teams  
> - Align with business needs  
> - Design for flexibility and enforcement  

> [!danger]  
> Risks:  
> - Over-retained data increases breach exposure  
> - Expired data should be deleted or replaced  
> - Cloud retention settings must be automated and enforced  

### ✅ Benefits
- Reduces liability  
- Ensures compliance  
- Improves data lifecycle management
